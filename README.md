# Telecom Cybersecurity Portfolio

> **Cybersecurity-focused portfolio of a telecommunications engineering specializing in network security, protocol analysis, and ethical hacking within telecom environments (4G/5G, VoIP, SS7/Diameter).**

This repository showcases my hands-on projects, research notes, tools, and reports developed during my studies and self-directed learning in cybersecurity — with a strong emphasis on the unique threats and defense strategies in modern telecommunications infrastructure.

---

## 🔍 About This Portfolio

As a telecom engineering student passionate about securing critical communication networks, I explore the intersection of **telecom protocols**, **network penetration testing**, and **offensive/defensive security**. This portfolio reflects my journey from foundational networking concepts to advanced topics like:

- **Telecom-specific attack surfaces** (SS7, Diameter, GTP, VoLTE)
- **Traffic analysis and anomaly detection**
- **Active host discovery in telecom environments**
- **Threat modeling for 4G/5G core networks**
- **Security hardening of telecom infrastructure**

All projects are designed to bridge academic knowledge with real-world security challenges faced by mobile network operators (MNOs), ISPs, and critical infrastructure providers.

## 📁 Key Projects & Artifacts

- **`telecom_traffic_analysis.md`**  
  In-depth analysis of mobile network traffic captures (PCAPs), focusing on identifying misconfigurations, unencrypted signaling, and potential interception points in 4G/5G control planes.

- **`active_hosts.txt`**  
  Results from network reconnaissance in lab environments — identifying live hosts, open ports, and services typical in telecom testbeds (e.g., HSS, MME, PGW interfaces).

- **`project_report.txt`**  
  Technical report on a simulated telecom penetration test, including methodology, findings (e.g., exposed Diameter interfaces), and mitigation recommendations.

- **`resume.md`**  
  My cybersecurity-focused resume — highlighting skills in Wireshark, Nmap, Metasploit, telecom protocol analysis, and relevant coursework/certifications (e.g., CompTIA Security+, eJPT).

- **Security Research Notes**  
  Ongoing documentation of telecom vulnerabilities, CVEs, and protocol weaknesses (e.g., SS7 location tracking, GTP firewall bypasses).
